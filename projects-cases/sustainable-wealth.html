Sustainable Wealth for Guyana: Building Enabling Conditions for a Circular Economy Model of the town of Bartica.

Summary of the project.

Project: Creating Sustainable Wealth for Guyana: Building Enabling Conditions for a Circular Economy Model of Bartica
Timeline of the Project (diagnostics and CE proposal) January 2018-December 2018
Next phase (implementation) January 2019---in standby
Project Goal (proposed for post pandemic) Transform the current linear gold mining industry into a circular value ecosystem to re-valuate waste (tailings) and residues of the operation (tires, metal scrap, plastics, etc.) from the mining industry into valuable goods, generating new economic support for the city of Bartica.
Led by United Nations Environmental Program (UNEP); SWIT Group, and the city of Bartica.
Country and Region of implementation of this project/initiative Bartica, British Guyana
United Nation SDGs covered in this project/initiative SDG#8: Decent work and Economic growth for the community. Once implemented, the project will create sustainable economic profits through new startups.
SDG#9: Industry, innovation, and infrastructure. The design of the model promotes inclusive and sustainable industrialization.
SDG#11: Sustainable Cities and Communities because the project will enable the reduction of waste in the city. Waste generated in the city will be given a new value.
The goal of the project is to create economic profits through new entrepreneurships from the recycling of all residues from the gold industry.
